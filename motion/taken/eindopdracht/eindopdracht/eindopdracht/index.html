Pyramids

Egypt's Great Pyramid of Giza, which stands 481 feet (147 meters) high, is the world's tallest pyramid.

It was built in the third millennium B.C. and is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

But the world's largest pyramid lies half a world away in Cholula, Mexico. Tlachihualtepetl, or the Great Pyramid of Cholula

stands 217 feet (66 meters) tall, but it measures 1,480 by 1,480 feet (450 by 450 meters) at its base, nearly four times larger than Giza's pyramid.

It's also the world's largest monument, as well as the largest Pre-Columbian monument in the Americas.

It was built from the third century B.C. to the ninth century A.D. by a succession of four civilizations, including the Aztecs.
